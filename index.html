GRADUATION

kanYeWest | © 2007

One of my favorite Kanye albums, Graduation is the final installment of Kanye's original album trilogy, preceded by The College Dropout and Late Registration. I chose this album over the rest of Kanye's extensive discography as I feel that though some of his albums may have better songs, such as Ghost Town from ye or Runaway from My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy, this album best showcases the talent of Kanye's early years as an artist, while also having—in my opinion—the highest proportion of great songs.

Some of the promotional materials for the album, photographed in Times Square sometime during 2007.

Graduation also has my favorite album cover of any of Kanye's work: a contemporary Japanese piece by the artist Takashi Murakami, whose other art I can find on some mugs in my own home.

On the right: an exibit for Takashi Murakami in San Francisco's Asian Art Museum. On the left: one of Murakami's more recognizable pieces.

Released on September 11th, 2007, this album serves as a transition piece from the more soulful tunes of Kanye's first two albums to the more powerful music seen on 808s and Heartbreaks and My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy. The song debuted at number one on Billboard's Top 200, selling nearly a million copies in the first week of its release. It has since septupled these sales, and has been certified as quintuple platinum.

Graphic from the Billboard 200 showing West's Graduation at number one during its first week on the chart.

The hit single Stronger was also released with this album, topping the Billboard Hot 100 in the US as well as 10 other countries. In the years since its release, the album has gained considerable fame and been granted many prestigious musical awards. The album has been included on Billboard's Top 500 albums of all time, \though notably at a higher spot than Kanye's later My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy, which currently sits at #17 overall. Graduation also earned a Grammy for Best Rap Album of the Year, and was nominated for the Best Album of the Year. Overall, I find the music on this album to be very good, both on its own and in the context of Kanye's discography, showing a major transition in the sound that he tries to cultivate.
