GRADUATION

kanYeWest | © 2007

One of my favorite Kanye albums, Graduation is the final installment of Kanye's original album trilogy, preceded by The College Dropout and Late Registration. I chose this album over the rest of Kanye's extensive discography as I feel that though some of his albums may have better songs, such as Ghost Town from ye or Runaway from My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy, this album best showcases the talent of Kanye's early years as an artist, while also having—in my opinion—the highest proportion of great songs.

Some of the promotional materials for the album, photographed in Times Square sometime during 2007.

Graduation also has my favorite album cover of any of Kanye's work: a contemporary Japanese piece by the artist Takashi Murakami. Outside of this album cover, I enjoy a large portion of his other works, and even have a mug with some of his designs on it, purchased when my family visited an exhibit of his while in San Francisco.

On the right: an exibit for Takashi Murakami in San Francisco's Asian Art Museum. On the left: one of Murakami's more recognizable pieces.

Released on September 11th, 2007, Graduation serves as a transition piece from the more soulful tunes of Kanye's first two albums to the more powerful music seen on 808s and Heartbreaks and My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy. The song debuted at number one on Billboard's Top 200, selling nearly a million copies in the first week of its release. It has since septupled these sales, and has been certified as quintuple platinum.

Graphic from the Billboard 200 showing West's Graduation at number one during its first week on the chart.

The hit single Stronger was also released with this album, topping the Billboard Hot 100 in the US as well as 10 other countries. In the years since its release, the album has gained considerable fame and been granted many prestigious musical awards, being included on Billboard's Top 500 albums of all time. Moreover, Graduation earned a Grammy for Best Rap Album of the Year, and was nominated for the Best Album of the Year.

West receiving his Grammy for Best Rap Album in 2008, with his acceptance speech paying tribute to his recently deceased mother Donda.

Overall, I find the music on this album to be very good, both on its own and in the context of Kanye's discography, showing a major transition in the sound that he tries to cultivate. Below this block of text, I have provided three rankings regarding this album: the first is the track list as provided on the CD, the second is my personal ranking of all songs, and the third is all songs ranked by how many streams they have on Spotify. Some of my favorite songs on this album include I Wonder and Homecoming, as I feel they hold some of the best narrative structures on the album, while both showcasing Kanye's sampling and producing talents in completely different ways. One of my other favorites off this album is The Glory, which I feel is a somewhat unusual take, especially when considering how often this song has been streamed compared to all other songs on the album. However, what I find truly great about Graduation is that I still enjoy some of the lowest ranked songs on the album, and that I feel that these songs still demonstrate the breadth of Kanye's artistry. If you have not yet listened to this album, I would encourage you to experience the melodies for yourself. I promise you, it will certainly be worth your time!
